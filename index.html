A Tribute to Meanie the Cat

Meanie (or Mean Mr. Mustard, Meaniebaby, Poopybutt, Bubs, or Bubbyboy) was a fine friend to have around for 10 years.

I picked up this orange and white fluffball from a friend of a friend with kittens, he was 2 months old, covered in fleas, and undoubtedly the most handsome of the litter. They called him “Shy Guy.” He evolved into a cat who was quite the opposite. He napped belly-up on the front porch. He went after dogs, unprovoked. Meanie sprinted (as much as a round boy can sprint) to and from the door when it was time for him to go outside- his preferred place to make a poop. He would wake me up by climbing all fourteen pounds of pure beefy cat onto my chest when it was time for face scratches. And he got all of the scratches. If you’d rub his lower back, right above his tail, Meanie would stop whatever he was doing and have no choice but to incessantly lick his front paw until you let up- my favorite party trick. He loved his sister Phoebe too- allowing her to be his little spoon whenever she sought to steal his body heat.

In his later years, he loved sunbathing on the porch, watching people, birds and bugs, and giving death stares to dogs of any stature. Mostly, I’ll miss his regal sits on my lap in front of the tv, purring loudly next to me in bed, and watching his proper trot whenever he heard dry food spilling into his cat bowl. Love you Bubbyboy.
